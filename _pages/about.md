I am a security engineer with a long-standing research interest in adversary simulation, offensive security, and the application of AI to cybersecurity problems.

My earlier research explored automated adversary simulation using deep reinforcement learning, completed as part of my MSc in Information Security at Royal Holloway, University of London. That work evaluated RL agents in both simulated and emulated network environments, using the CybORG benchmark and custom AWS-deployed infrastructure controlled via the Metasploit Framework.

My current research focuses on LLM-based pentesting agents — how they are architected, how they perform across benchmark environments, and how their capabilities are evolving. A central part of this work involves reproducing and analysing existing research systems such as CyBench to build a rigorous, comparative picture of the state of the field.

This site serves as the public index for that ongoing work.
